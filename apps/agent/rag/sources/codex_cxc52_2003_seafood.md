# Codex Alimentarius CXC 52-2003 — Code of Practice for Fish and Fishery Products

**Source:** Codex Alimentarius Commission
**Document:** CAC/RCP 52-2003, Revised 2013
**Amendment Date:** 2013-01-01
**Applicable Categories:** seafood, rte, cold_chain, general

---

## 1. Scope

This Code of Practice applies to **fish, shellfish, and other aquatic animals** prepared for human consumption. It covers:
- Fresh and frozen finfish and shellfish
- Processed and RTE fish products (smoked, cured, dried)
- Aquaculture products destined for human consumption
- Products for further processing and for retail

Specific chemical hazards unique to seafood — **histamine (scombrotoxin)**, **marine biotoxins (PSP, ASP, DSP)**, and **heavy metals** — are addressed with dedicated CCP requirements.

---

## 2. Primary Production and Harvesting

### 2.1 Fishing Vessel Hygiene
- All surfaces of vessel holds, deck areas, and equipment that contact fish must be of smooth, non-absorbent, corrosion-resistant material
- Potable water or clean seawater used for rinsing fish; potable water used for processing
- Fish must be iced immediately after capture: **critical requirement** — delay in icing > 1 hour in tropical temperatures (>30°C) significantly increases histamine risk

### 2.2 Histamine Formation — Time/Temperature CCP
**Histamine (Scombrotoxin) Hazard — Species-Specific:**
Applies to: tuna, mackerel, sardine, herring, anchovies, amberjack, mahi-mahi, and related scombroid fish

| Condition | Histamine Accumulation Rate |
|---|---|
| Fish at 0°C (ice) | Negligible accumulation |
| Fish at 5°C | Slow accumulation (< 50 ppm in 24h) |
| Fish at 10°C | Moderate (< 50 ppm in 8h) |
| Fish at 21°C (ambient tropical) | Rapid (> 200 ppm in 6h) |
| Fish at 30°C | Very rapid (> 500 ppm in 3h) |

**CCP — Chilling of scombroid fish:**
- **Critical limit:** Fish must be chilled to ≤ 4°C within 2 hours of capture or death
- Monitoring: Ice status (ice coverage > 80% of fish surface); probe temperature log; time-from-catch records
- Histamine once formed is **NOT destroyed by cooking or freezing** — prevention is the only control

### 2.3 Marine Biotoxins — Harvesting Area Control
**Hazards:**
- **Paralytic Shellfish Poison (PSP):** Produced by dinoflagellate Alexandrium; in bivalve shellfish (oysters, clams, mussels)
- **Amnesic Shellfish Poison (ASP):** Domoic acid from diatom Pseudo-nitzschia; in bivalves, anchovies, crabs
- **Diarrhetic Shellfish Poison (DSP):** Okadaic acid from Dinophysis; in bivalves

**CCP — Area closure / monitoring:**
- **Critical limit:** PSP ≤ 800 μg/100 g shellfish meat (Codex standard; stricter country limits may apply)
- ASP: Domoic acid ≤ 20 mg/kg meat
- Shellfish harvesting area must be covered by a government-approved monitoring program; closure orders must be followed
- No in-plant CCP can substitute for harvest area control — biotoxins are heat-stable

## 3. Receiving, Handling, and Cold Chain

### 3.1 Receiving Inspection — CCP
**Parameters to check at receiving:**
| Parameter | Acceptable | Rejection Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature (chilled finfish) | ≤ 4°C | > 7°C at core |
| Temperature (frozen seafood) | ≤ -18°C | > -12°C |
| Odour (fresh fish) | Fresh seaweed/sea odour | Ammonia, TMA, rotten, or sour odour |
| Eyes (fresh fish) | Bright, clear, protruding | Sunken, milky, cloudy |
| Gills (fresh fish) | Bright red | Brown, grey, or dry gills |
| Histamine (tuna, mackerel) | < 100 ppm (screening) | > 100 ppm on screening = reject lot |

### 3.2 Ice Usage
- Flake ice or tube ice must be made from potable water; ice storage must be covered and hygienic
- **Ratio:** Minimum 1:1 ice to fish by weight for whole fish; 2:1 for pre-gutted fish held > 24 hours
- Drain melt water must be discarded; recirculated ice water is only acceptable for primary cooling in controlled systems

### 3.3 Frozen Seafood Storage — CCP
- **Critical limit:** Frozen storage temperature ≤ -18°C continuously
- Temperature alarm system: triggers at > -15°C
- Monitoring: Continuous data logger; review weekly; calibration annually

## 4. Processing Operations

### 4.1 Filleting and Skinning

**Biological hazards:**
- Personnel hygiene is critical — no glove removal or reuse without sanitation between fish
- Filleting table surfaces: stainless steel; sanitize every 4 hours during production
- Cross-contamination prevention: separate lines for different species (especially for allergen labelling)

**Physical hazards — Bone fragments:**
- Pin-bone removal: manual or mechanical; residual bone is a physical hazard
- **Control:** Electronic bone detector (x-ray or optical) recommended for boneless products labeled "boneless"
- Candling (light table) for some fish types to detect parasites

### 4.2 Parasites in Fish

**Relevant parasites:**
- Anisakis spp. (roundworms): in herring, cod, mackerel, salmon, squid; larvae survive refrigeration but killed by freezing
- Diphyllobothrium (tapeworm): in freshwater fish (pike, perch); killed by cooking ≥ 60°C or freezing
- Cryptosporidium: rare in marine fish; relevant in freshwater aquaculture

**CCP — Parasite control:**
- **Method 1 (Freezing):** -20°C for ≥ 7 days (all tissue), or -35°C for ≥ 15 hours — kills all Anisakis larvae
  - For raw or lightly processed fish (sushi, gravlax, marinated): MANDATORY freezing
- **Method 2 (Cooking):** ≥ 60°C throughout the flesh for ≥ 1 minute — kills all parasites
- Visual inspection alone is NOT acceptable as the sole control measure

### 4.3 Cooking of Fish and Shellfish — CCP

| Fish Product | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Finfish fillets | ≥ 63°C internal for ≥ 15 seconds |
| Whole fish | ≥ 63°C at geometric center |
| Shrimp/prawns | ≥ 63°C internal; shell turns pink throughout |
| Oysters (cooked) | ≥ 90°C internal or equivalent |
| Crab and lobster | ≥ 74°C internal |

### 4.4 Cold-Smoked Fish (Salmon, Trout, Mackerel)

Cold-smoked fish is NOT a cooked product; Listeria monocytogenes survives the smoking process.

**Combined process controls (multi-hurdle):**
1. **Water activity (Aw):** Brine/salt to achieve Aw ≤ 0.97 in final product
2. **Smoking temperature:** 25–35°C (cold smoke); does not eliminate Listeria
3. **Post-process chilling:** ≤ 3°C immediately after smoking; critical for Listeria control
4. **Shelf life management:** Validated shelf life at storage temperature; L. monocytogenes growth modelling required

**Listeria environmental monitoring for cold-smoked facilities:**
- Zone 1 (direct product contact): Monthly testing; zero positive tolerance
- Zone 2 (near-line): Monthly testing
- Zone 3 (general facility): Quarterly testing

### 4.5 Dried and Cured Fish

**Water activity control — CCP:**
- For products dependent on reduced water activity for microbiological safety:
- **Critical limit:** Aw ≤ 0.85 to prevent Staphylococcal toxin formation; Aw ≤ 0.70 for shelf-stable products
- Monitoring: Calibrated water activity meter on each batch; frequency based on risk assessment

## 5. Allergen Management in Seafood

**Major allergens in seafood:**
- Fish (any species): Any fish protein (e.g., parvalbumin) is an allergen
- Crustaceans (shrimp, crab, lobster): Tropomyosin is the major allergen; heat-stable
- Molluscs (oysters, clams, mussels, squid): Recognized allergen in many countries

**Controls:**
- Dedicated processing lines for allergen-containing products; full washdown (validated) required between species if shared equipment used
- Allergen labelling: Must declare species of fish and type of shellfish
- Cross-contact risk: Shared fryer oil, shared cooking equipment → allergen advisory labelling required

## 6. Microbiological Standards for Seafood

Aligned with FSSAI 2024 standards (see cross-reference document):

| Product | Organism | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| Fresh/chilled finfish | Total Viable Count | ≤ 10⁶ CFU/g |
| Fresh finfish | Salmonella | Absent/25g |
| Cooked shrimp | Total Aerobic Count | ≤ 10⁵ CFU/g |
| Raw shrimp (tropical) | Vibrio parahaemolyticus | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| All seafood | Vibrio cholerae O1, O139 | Absent/25g |
| RTE seafood | Listeria monocytogenes | Absent/25g |

**Histamine limits (FSSAI/Codex):**
| Product Stage | Maximum Histamine |
|---|---|
| At production (FDA/FSSAI) | ≤ 50 ppm |
| At retail/import | ≤ 200 ppm (Codex); ≤ 100 ppm (FDA) |
| Action level (FSSAI) | > 200 ppm = lot condemnation |
