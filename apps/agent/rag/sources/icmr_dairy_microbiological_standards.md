# FSSAI & ICMR Microbiological Standards for Dairy Products

## FSSAI Appendix B - Microbiological Standards
The Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) enforces strict microbiological limits under Appendix B of the FSS (Food Products Standards and Food Additives) Regulations to ensure dairy safety across India. The regulatory limits for Standard Plate Count (SPC), Coliforms, Salmonella, and Listeria monocytogenes depend heavily on whether the dairy product is raw, pasteurized, or further processed.

### Sampling Plan Definition
FSSAI employs a three-class sampling plan (n, c, m, M) at the manufacturing level to evaluate food hygiene and safety:
- **n**: Number of sample units to be collected from the batch.
- **c**: Maximum allowable number of marginally acceptable sample units (between m and M).
- **m**: Satisfactory limit; values below this are satisfactory.
- **M**: Absolute ceiling limit; any value above M results in batch rejection.

## Liquid Milk Standards

### Raw Milk (At Collection/Procurement)
- Standard Plate Count (SPC / TPC): Maximum 5 × 10⁵ CFU/mL (M)
- Coliform Count: Maximum 1 × 10³ CFU/mL (M)
- Salmonella spp.: Absent in 25 mL
- Listeria monocytogenes: Absent in 25 mL

### Pasteurized Liquid Milk (Direct Consumption)
- Standard Plate Count (SPC / TPC): m = 30,000 CFU/mL, M = 50,000 CFU/mL
- Coliform Count: m = Absent in 0.1 mL, M = 10 CFU/mL
- Salmonella spp.: Absent in 25 mL
- Listeria monocytogenes: Absent in 25 mL

## Processed Dairy Products

### Milk Powder (Infant / Skimmed)
- Standard Plate Count (SPC / TPC): m = 10,000 CFU/g, M = 50,000 CFU/g
- Coliform Count: m = Absent in 0.1 g, M = 10 CFU/g
- Salmonella spp.: Absent in 25 g
- Listeria monocytogenes: Absent in 25 g

### Cheese (Hard / Semi-hard)
- Standard Plate Count (SPC / TPC): m = 50,000 CFU/g (M)
- Coliform Count: m = Nil / Absent (M)
- Salmonella spp.: Absent in 25 g
- Listeria monocytogenes: Absent in 25 g

## Parameters and Safety Significance

### Standard Plate Count (SPC)
Serves as a primary indicator of overall structural hygiene, baseline bacterial load, and shelf-life potential. Pasteurized pouch milk must consistently hit <30,000 CFU/mL to be deemed satisfactory. High SPC values signal failure in the pasteurization temperature-time loop or poor post-pasteurization cold chains.

### Coliform Count
Acts as a hygiene indicator organism pointing toward poor sanitation, fecal contamination, or processing failures. The absolute upper limit for retail pasteurized milk is 10 CFU/mL. Because coliforms are killed by pasteurization, their presence in packaged milk is treated as clear evidence of post-processing contamination.

### Salmonella spp.
Serious foodborne pathogen responsible for salmonellosis outbreak hazards. A strict Zero-Tolerance Policy applies. It must be entirely Absent in 25 mL (or grams) for every dairy category entering commercial streams.

### Listeria monocytogenes
Psychrotrophic pathogen that can dangerously multiply even at refrigeration temperatures (4°C to 8°C), posing high risks in ready-to-eat dairy like cheese. It must be Absent in 25 mL/g. Processors are required to monitor facilities to eliminate Listeria niches in wet dairy environments.

## Authorized Testing Methodologies
Compliance testing must strictly align with the methodologies authorized in the FSSAI Manual of Methods of Analysis for Foods - Microbiology and corresponding Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS / IS) codes:
- **SPC**: Evaluated using Plate Count Agar (PCA) incubated at 30°C for 72 hours (Method matching IS: 5402 / ISO 4833).
- **Coliforms**: Enumerated using Violet Red Bile Agar (VRBA) or MacConkey Broth (IS: 5401 / ISO 4832).
- **Pathogens (Salmonella & Listeria)**: Tested using selective enrichment followed by diagnostic plating or validated rapid PCR toolkits (IS: 5887 series).
