# FSSAI Schedule 4 Part II — Food Business Operators: Transporters and Cold Chain

**Source:** Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI)
**Regulation:** Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule 4, Part II
**Amendment Date:** 2024-01-01
**Applicable Categories:** cold_chain, dairy_pasteurized, meat, seafood, rte

---

## 1. Scope and Applicability

Part II of Schedule 4 applies to all Food Business Operators (FBOs) engaged in the **transportation of food products**, including:
- Refrigerated transport of perishable goods (dairy, meat, seafood, RTE products)
- Ambient temperature transport of packaged and processed foods
- Cold storage facilities acting as intermediaries in the food supply chain
- Last-mile delivery operators for perishable food categories

All vehicles, containers, and cold chain infrastructure used for food transport must comply with these requirements.

---

## 2. Temperature Control Requirements During Transport

### 2.1 Chilled Products
- Products requiring chilling (0–5°C) must be transported in pre-cooled refrigerated vehicles
- **Critical limit:** Cargo compartment temperature must remain ≤ 5°C throughout transit
- Pre-cooling of vehicle before loading is mandatory — vehicle must reach set-point temperature before food is loaded
- Temperature must be monitored **continuously** using calibrated data loggers; minimum one data logger per compartment
- Maximum permissible temperature excursion: ≤ 2°C above upper limit, ≤ 30 minutes cumulative duration

### 2.2 Frozen Products
- **Critical limit:** Cargo temperature must remain ≤ -18°C for frozen products
- Dry ice or mechanical refrigeration is acceptable; gel packs are NOT acceptable for frozen products during transit > 4 hours
- Ice cream and frozen desserts: ≤ -23°C during transport
- Deviation trigger: Any reading above -15°C for more than 15 minutes requires product hold and assessment

### 2.3 Hot Foods (Food Service Distribution)
- **Critical limit:** Hot food must be maintained at ≥ 60°C during transit
- Maximum transit time for hot food: 2 hours from point of cooking to point of service
- Insulated containers must demonstrate ≥ 60°C retention over 2 hours (annual validation required)

### 2.4 Pasteurized Dairy Products
- Pasteurized milk and dairy products: transport at 0–4°C
- Maximum break in cold chain: 30 minutes cumulative during loading/unloading
- Products found above 7°C at delivery point must be rejected

## 3. Vehicle and Container Hygiene Requirements

### 3.1 Vehicle Construction
- Internal surfaces of food compartments must be smooth, non-porous, non-absorbent, and washable
- Stainless steel or food-grade polymer linings required for direct-contact surfaces
- Drainage points must be sealable to prevent pest ingress during loading
- Refrigeration units must not share fuel with the driving cab ventilation

### 3.2 Cleaning and Disinfection
- Cold chain vehicles: wash-down after every load; full chemical sanitization minimum **twice per week**
- Cleaning records must be maintained including: date, vehicle registration, cleaning chemical used, concentration, and employee signature
- Pre-trip inspection checklist must include hygiene verification by supervisor
- **CCP — Vehicle sanitation:** ATP bioluminescence testing or equivalent at least monthly; target: <100 RLU

### 3.3 Pest and Contamination Control
- No raw agricultural commodities may be co-transported with RTE or pasteurized products in the same compartment
- Vehicles that transport non-food chemicals must undergo full decontamination and FSSAI verification before food use
- Rodent exclusion seals must be intact on all entry points

## 4. Cold Storage Facilities

### 4.1 Facility Requirements
- All cold storage facilities used in the food supply chain must be licensed under FSSAI
- Temperature mapping of the entire facility must be performed at commissioning and annually thereafter
- **No warm zone** should exceed the product temperature limit; mapping must confirm all zones are within ±1°C of set-point

### 4.2 Critical Control Points in Cold Storage
| CCP | Parameter | Critical Limit | Monitoring Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Receiving dock | Product temperature at intake | Chilled: ≤ 5°C; Frozen: ≤ -15°C | Every consignment |
| Storage zone temperature | Air temperature | Chilled: 0–5°C; Frozen: ≤ -18°C | Continuous, auto-alert |
| Dispatch door | Time product spends above limit | ≤ 15 min per consignment | Every consignment |

### 4.3 Power Failure Protocol
- Backup power (generator) must be available and tested monthly
- **Written procedure** for power failure: products in frozen storage remain acceptable if temperature stays ≤ -12°C and power is restored within 4 hours
- FIFO (First In, First Out) stock rotation is mandatory in all cold stores

---

## 5. Personnel Hygiene and Training

- All drivers and loaders handling food must hold a valid FSSAI Food Hygiene Training Certificate (minimum Level 1)
- Personal protective equipment (gloves, clean overalls) mandatory when handling exposed food products
- Persons with food-borne illness symptoms must not handle food or operate food transport vehicles
- Medical fitness certificates required annually

---

## 6. Documentation and Record Keeping

### Required Records (minimum 2 years retention)
1. **Temperature logs:** Continuous data logger records for every journey; time-stamped
2. **Vehicle sanitation records:** Every cleaning event
3. **Product receipt/dispatch records:** Product name, quantity, temperature at receipt and dispatch, vehicle registration
4. **Deviation records:** Any temperature excursion with corrective action taken
5. **Calibration certificates:** For all thermometers and data loggers (annual)
6. **Driver training certificates:** Updated annually

### 6.1 Traceability Requirements
- Each consignment must be traceable within 24 hours from receipt notification to source and all onward distribution points
- Lot/batch numbers must be recorded on all transit documentation
- FSSAI License Number of origin FBO must appear on all accompanying documentation

## 7. Hazard Analysis for Cold Chain Operations

### Biological Hazards
| Hazard | Source | Control Measure | CCP? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Listeria monocytogenes proliferation | Temperature abuse during chilled transport | Temperature ≤ 5°C throughout; monitoring | YES — CCP |
| Salmonella spp. in RTE products | Cross-contamination from raw products | Segregation; separate compartments | YES — CCP |
| Psychrotrophic pathogen growth (Yersinia, Pseudomonas) | Extended transit time + chilled transport | Time/temperature control; max transit time limits | YES — CCP |
| Clostridium perfringens in hot-held foods | Temperature drop during hot food transport | Maintain ≥ 60°C; 2-hour maximum | YES — CCP |

### Chemical Hazards
| Hazard | Source | Control Measure | CCP? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Refrigerant gas contamination | Leaking refrigeration unit | Regular maintenance; leak detection | YES — CCP |
| Cleaning chemical residues | Improper rinsing after wash-down | Triple rinse protocol; ATP testing | NO — PRP |

### Physical Hazards
| Hazard | Source | Control Measure | CCP? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Metal fragments | Damaged vehicle interior | Pre-trip inspection; metal detector at dispatch | NO — PRP |
| Glass contamination | Broken temperature gauges | Plastic-faced gauges; inspection protocol | NO — PRP |
