## 1. Introduction

The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR), in alignment with FAO/WHO Codex standards, has established microbiological guidelines for meat and fishery products to protect consumers from foodborne illness. These are supplemented by FSSAI product-specific standards under the FSS (Food Products Standards and Food Additives) Regulations, 2011.

## 3. Critical Control Points for Meat Processing — ICMR Recommendations

### 3.1 Slaughter Hygiene

- Post-slaughter bacterial contamination primarily originates from: hide/fleece, intestinal contents, air in slaughter hall, handlers
- **Target:** Carcass surface TPC <3 log CFU/cm² immediately after dressing (before chilling)
- Steam vacuuming and organic acid (lactic acid 2% spray) shown to reduce surface TPC by 1.5–2 log CFU/cm²

### 3.2 Chilling — Most Critical Step

- **ICMR recommendation:** Chilling is the single most important CCP for fresh meat safety
- Temperature of slaughter hall: ≤12°C to limit bacterial growth during dressing
- Rapid chilling: carcass from 37°C to ≤7°C core within 24 hours (large animals), 4 hours (poultry)
- Psychrotrophic pathogen growth rates:
  - L. monocytogenes: doubling time 40 hours at 4°C; 12 hours at 10°C
  - Y. enterocolitica: doubling time 20 hours at 4°C; 8 hours at 10°C

### 3.3 Cooking (CCP for processed/cooked meat)

| Product Type | Internal Temperature | Hold Time |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------| 
| Whole muscle beef/lamb | ≥72°C | 15 seconds |
| Ground beef (burgers) | ≥72°C throughout | 15 seconds |
| Poultry — all parts | ≥80°C | 15 seconds |
| Pork products | ≥72°C | 15 seconds |
| Stuffed products | ≥75°C at centre | 15 seconds |

| Canned fish | Commercially sterile | — | — | — | — | — |

### 4.2 Histamine in Scombroid Fish (CRITICAL)

- Scombroid fish (tuna, mackerel, sardines, herring) — risk of histamine formation from bacterial decarboxylation of histidine
- **FSSAI Maximum Limit:** Histamine ≤100 mg/kg in fresh/chilled scombroid fish; ≤200 mg/kg for fermented/salted fish products
- **Critical Limit for Histamine CCP:** ≤50 mg/kg (recommended pre-harvest, immediate chilling)
- Control: Immediate chilling to ≤4°C after catch; unbroken cold chain; rejection of fish with elevated biogenic amines

## 7. Parasitic Hazards (Critical for Meat)

| Parasite | Primary Host | Infective Stage | Control Method |
|---------|------------|----------------|---------------|
| Taenia saginata | Cattle | Cysticercus bovis in muscle | Post-mortem inspection; thorough cooking ≥65°C |
| Taenia solium | Pig | Cysticercus cellulosae in muscle | Post-mortem inspection; cooking ≥72°C |
| Trichinella spiralis | Pig, wild boar | Larvae in muscle | Digestion test; cooking ≥71°C or freezing −18°C for 20 days |
| Anisakis spp. | Marine fish | Larvae in flesh | Freezing −18°C ≥72h; cooking ≥60°C |
| Cryptosporidium | All ruminants | Oocysts in water/surface | Potable water; good hygiene |
