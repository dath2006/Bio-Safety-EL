# FSSAI Food Safety and Standards (Packaging and Labelling) Regulations — Overview

**Source:** Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI)
**Regulation:** Food Safety and Standards (Packaging and Labelling) Regulations, 2011 with amendments up to 2023
**Amendment Date:** 2023-08-01
**Applicable Categories:** packaged_food, beverages, dairy, dairy_pasteurized, meat, seafood, rte, spices

---

## 1. Scope

These regulations apply to **all pre-packaged food products** sold in India, including domestically produced and imported goods. Key requirements include mandatory labelling, allergen declaration, use-by dating, nutritional information, and food safety-related packaging standards.

---

## 2. Mandatory Label Declarations

### 2.1 Core Declarations (All Packaged Foods)
Every package of food for sale in India must display:

1. **Name of food** — generic and/or brand name; must not mislead
2. **List of ingredients** — in descending order of weight; water must be included
3. **Net quantity** — weight, volume, or count; in metric units (grams, mL, number)
4. **FSSAI License Number** — of the manufacturer/packer
5. **Batch/Lot number** — for traceability
6. **Date marking:** "Best Before" OR "Use By" as appropriate (see Section 2.2)
7. **Nutritional Information** — mandatory for most categories (see Section 2.4)
8. **Country of Origin** — for imported products
9. **Name and address** — of manufacturer, packer, or importer

### 2.2 Date Marking — Critical Food Safety Declaration

**Use By Date** (for highly perishable products):
- Required when products are microbiologically unsafe after a specific date
- Examples: fresh pasteurized milk, fresh meat, fresh cut produce, RTE chilled foods
- Format: "USE BY DD/MM/YYYY"
- **After the use-by date, the product MUST NOT be sold or consumed**

**Best Before Date** (for products with quality deterioration but not safety risk):
- Required for all other packaged foods
- Examples: packaged spices, biscuits, UHT milk, frozen foods, packaged beverages
- Format: "BEST BEFORE DD/MM/YYYY" or "BEST BEFORE END MM/YYYY"
- Products may be safe but quality may deteriorate after this date

**Shelf Life Validation Requirements:**
- FBO must conduct or commission validated shelf life studies
- Studies must test microbial, chemical, and physical stability
- For RTE products claiming extended shelf life: challenge testing with Listeria monocytogenes or other relevant pathogens is required

### 2.3 Storage Instructions
- All labels must include **storage conditions** linked to date marking
- Examples: "Keep refrigerated at 0–5°C", "Store in a cool, dry place", "Best consumed within 3 days of opening"
- If product requires temperature control, the storage instruction is a **safety-critical declaration**

### 2.4 Nutritional Information Requirements

**Mandatory nutrients per 100g/100mL:**
| Nutrient | Required? | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Energy (kJ and kcal) | YES | |
| Total protein | YES | |
| Total carbohydrate | YES | |
| Total fat | YES | |
| Saturated fat | YES | |
| Trans fat | YES | Maximum 2g/100g total fat for packaged foods |
| Total sugar | YES | |

## 3. Allergen Labelling — Food Safety Critical

### 3.1 Mandatory Allergen Declarations

The following 8 major allergen categories must be declared on all food labels in India:

1. **Cereals containing gluten** — wheat, rye, barley, oats, and their hybridized strains
2. **Crustacean shellfish** — shrimp, crab, lobster, prawn
3. **Eggs** — from all poultry
4. **Fish** — all species of finfish
5. **Peanuts** (groundnuts)
6. **Soybeans**
7. **Tree nuts** — almonds, cashews, walnuts, pecans, pistachios, macadamia, hazelnuts, Brazil nuts, pine nuts
8. **Milk** — from all mammals; includes lactose

**Declaration format:** "Contains: Milk, Peanuts, Wheat" — prominently displayed, separate from ingredient list or highlighted within it (bold, underlined, or different font)

### 3.2 May Contain / Cross-Contact Advisory
- "May contain traces of [allergen]" — advisory labelling for cross-contact risk
- Must be based on a **Allergen Risk Assessment** — not used as a catch-all disclaimer without real risk
- Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) controls for allergens must be documented

### 3.3 Allergen Management in Manufacturing
**CCP for allergens (in shared production facilities):**
- Allergen changeover cleaning must be **validated** to demonstrate removal to below detectable levels
- Method: ELISA or lateral flow kits for target allergen; sensitivity ≤ 1 ppm for major allergens
- **Scheduling control:** Allergen-containing products scheduled at end of production run before full washdown
- **Documentation:** Allergen management plan required; reviewed annually

## 4. Packaging Safety Requirements

### 4.1 Food-Contact Packaging Materials

All packaging in direct contact with food must comply with:
- **IS 9845 (Plastics):** Migration limits for plastic food contact materials
- **IS 7347 (Paper/Board):** Permitted paper and board for food packaging

**Prohibited materials:**
- Recycled plastic for primary food packaging (direct contact) — only virgin-grade polymer permitted
- Newspaper, coloured paper for direct food wrapping
- Non-food-grade rubber seals or gaskets in contact with food

### 4.2 Migration Limits for Food Contact Plastics
| Substance | Maximum Migration Limit |
|---|---|
| Overall migration | ≤ 10 mg/dm² of packaging surface |
| Bisphenol A (BPA) | ≤ 0.6 mg/kg food |
| Heavy metals (Pb, Cd, Hg, Cr VI combined) | ≤ 100 ppm in packaging material |
| Vinyl Chloride Monomer | ≤ 1 ppb in food |
| Phthalates (DEHP, DBP) | ≤ 1.5 mg/kg food |

**Chemical CCP for packaging:** Supplier certification required; annual testing of high-risk packaging formats (fatty or acidic food contact)

### 4.3 Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) and Vacuum Packaging

**MAP gas combinations for food preservation:**
| Application | Gas Composition |
|---|---|
| Fresh red meat | 80% O2 / 20% CO2 (maintains red colour; O2 prevents Listeria aerobe inhibition) |
| Fresh poultry | 30% CO2 / 70% N2 |
| Fresh fish | 40% CO2 / 30% O2 / 30% N2 |
| RTE delicatessen | 30% CO2 / 70% N2 |
| Fresh pasta | 50% CO2 / 50% N2 |

**CCP for MAP — Gas composition control:**
- Gas ratio must be verified at line with inline gas analyzer; calibrated weekly
- **Critical limit:** CO2 concentration within ±5% of target; O2 within ±3% of target
- Seal integrity: 100% visual inspection + periodic destructive burst testing

**Vacuum Packaging and Clostridium botulinum risk:**
- Anaerobic, low-salt products: risk of Type E C. botulinum (non-proteolytic, grows at ≥ 3°C)
- **Control:** Maintain chilled chain ≤ 3°C OR include validated inhibitory formulation OR limit shelf life based on challenge study
- Advisory: vacuum-packed chilled fish at 3°C: shelf life ≤ 14 days without additional hurdles

## 5. Traceability and Recall

### 5.1 Traceability Requirements
All packaged food FBOs must:
- Maintain records identifying supplier of all ingredients and packaging materials
- Record batch/lot numbers of each production run
- Be able to trace any product to all customers within **4 hours** of regulatory request

### 5.2 Product Recall Procedures
- Written recall procedure required; tested annually
- Recall notification to FSSAI within **24 hours** of identifying a safety issue
- Public notification if product has reached retail level — coordination with FSSAI mandatory
- Corrective action report submitted within 30 days of recall completion

## 6. Special Labelling for High-Risk Categories

### 6.1 Raw Meat and Poultry
- Label must state: "KEEP REFRIGERATED — COOK THOROUGHLY BEFORE CONSUMPTION"
- Storage temperature on label
- Safe handling instructions: "Keep raw meat separate from other foods; wash hands after handling"

### 6.2 Shell Eggs
- "KEEP REFRIGERATED" mandatory in India for pasteurized/clean eggs
- "SELL BY" date: maximum 21 days from lay date

### 6.3 Honey
- No "Best Before" required (honey is shelf-stable); recommended labelling includes date of extraction
- Country of origin of honey must be declared

### 6.4 Infant Formula and Baby Foods
- Mandatory preparation instructions with safe water preparation statement
- No health claims permitted on infant formula packaging
- Stage labelling (Stage 1 = 0–6 months, Stage 2 = 6 months+) mandatory

---

## 7. Enforcement and Penalties

- Misbranded food products: FSS Act, 2006, Section 53 — penalty up to ₹3 lakh
- Sub-standard food products: Section 51 — penalty up to ₹5 lakh
- Unsafe/injurious food products: Section 57 — imprisonment up to 6 months and/or fine up to ₹1 lakh
- Adulterant rendering food injurious: Section 59 — imprisonment up to 7 years and fine up to ₹10 lakh
