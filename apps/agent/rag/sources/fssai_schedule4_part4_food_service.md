# FSSAI Schedule 4 Regulations for Catering and Food Service Establishments

## Scope and Applicability
Under Part V and Part VI of the Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011, specific sanitary and hygienic practices are mandated for all food service establishments, catering units, hotels, and restaurants operating in India.

## 1. Handling and Preparation Controls
- **Raw Material Source**: Food service operators must procure raw materials only from FSSAI-licensed/registered suppliers. Raw materials must be inspected upon receipt for freshness, temperature, and quality.
- **Cross-Contamination Prevention**: Strict separation of raw meats, poultry, and seafood from cooked and ready-to-eat foods must be maintained. Dedicated color-coded cutting boards and knives must be used:
  - **Red**: Raw meat
  - **Yellow**: Raw poultry
  - **Blue**: Raw seafood
  - **Green**: Fruits and vegetables
  - **White**: Dairy and ready-to-eat foods
- **Thawing Controls**: Thawing of frozen foods must be carried out in a refrigerator (below 4°C), under running potable water, or in a microwave oven immediately prior to cooking. Thawing at room temperature is strictly prohibited.

## 2. Temperature and Cooking Controls
To prevent pathogen multiplication, food service units must adhere to the following thermal boundaries:
- **Cooking Temperatures**: Cooked foods containing meat, poultry, or eggs must reach a minimum core temperature of **74°C** or above (or **70°C for 2 minutes**).
- **Hot Holding**: Cooked food held for service must be kept at a continuous temperature of **60°C or above**.
- **Cold Holding**: Refrigerated foods must be stored at **5°C or below**. Frozen food storage must be maintained at **-18°C or below**.
- **Cooling after Cooking**: Cooked food that is to be refrigerated must be cooled rapidly: from 60°C to 21°C within 2 hours, and then from 21°C to 5°C or below within an additional 4 hours.

## 3. Personal Hygiene and Sanitary Facilities
- **Health and Cleanliness**: Food handlers must undergo medical screening. Any handler showing symptoms of diarrhea, vomiting, skin infection, or fever must be excluded from food handling areas.
- **Handwashing Facilities**: Handwash stations equipped with non-hand operated taps, liquid disinfectant soap, paper towels or air dryers, and hand sanitizers must be provided at the entrance of processing and preparation areas.
- **Uniforms**: Food handlers must wear clean overalls, headgears enclosing all hair, and mouth masks while preparing or serving food.

## 4. Cleaning, Sanitation and Waste Management
- **Potable Water**: Potable water matching IS:10500 standards must be used for cooking, ice making, and cleaning utensils.
- **Cleaning Schedule**: Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for cleaning and sanitizing food contact surfaces, tables, and equipment must be established. All food contact surfaces must be sanitized with food-grade sanitizers.
- **Waste Disposal**: Waste bins must be kept closed, lined with disposable bags, and emptied regularly. Waste must be disposed of hygienically to prevent pest attraction.
- **Pest Control**: Regular, professional pest control treatments must be conducted, and records of treatments maintained.
