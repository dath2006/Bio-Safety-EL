# FSSAI Inspection Checklist for Food Businesses

## Overview of Audit Criteria
The FSSAI Milk Processing Inspection Checklist scores dairy processing establishments across major sub-sections based on Schedule 4 of the FSS (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011. Asterisk-marked (*) items are Critical Audit Points where failure immediately results in overall non-compliance.

## Audit Scoring and Grading Matrix
Inspectors allot marks (0, 1, or 2) based on compliance levels observed:
- **Compliance (C)**: Requirements are fully documented and seamlessly implemented. Full marks (2) awarded.
- **Partial Compliance (PC)**: Minor lapses present; system framework exists but lacks documentation (Not applicable for asterisk items). Half marks (1) awarded.
- **Non-Compliance (NC)**: Critical gap identified or zero execution observed. Zero marks (0) (Immediate failure if asterisk item).
- **Not Applicable (NA)**: Criteria does not apply to the specific plant's operational nature. Excluded from calculations.

### Final Plant Rating Criteria
- **≥ 85% Score**: Grade A (Exemplary Compliance / High Standard)
- **70% - 84% Score**: Grade B (Satisfactory Compliance)
- **50% - 69% Score**: Needs Improvement (Requires target-bound corrective actions)
- **< 50% Score**: Non-Compliant (Suspension risk or cancellation of Central License)

## Critical Audit Points (Asterisk-marked *)

### Water Potability (*)
Potable water matching IS:10500 standards must be used for processing, sanitation, and steam generation. Chemical and microbiological water testing records must be verified semi-annually.

### Pest Control Program (*)
A documented pest management system must be handled by trained, certified external agencies or personnel, with zero tolerance for visible infestations. Pest control logs, traps placement map, and chemicals MSDS must be maintained.

### Medical Fitness (*)
All food handlers must undergo annual medical examinations and be certified by a registered medical practitioner to be free from infectious/contagious diseases.

### GHP Controls (*)
Clean protective clothing, headgears, shoe covers, trimmed nails, and a strict ban on loose jewelry, smoking, or spitting in the processing bay are mandatory. Hand washing stations with disinfectant soap and sanitizers must be operational at entry points.

### FoSTaC Training (*)
At least one trained and certified Food Safety Supervisor must be available for every 25 food handlers on-site.

## Design & Facilities Infrastructure
- **No Residential Access**: The manufacturing area must not have direct access to residential spaces.
- **Unidirectional Flow**: The layout must ensure a strict layout flow (Raw Milk Receiving → Processing/Pasteurization → Packaging → Cold Storage) to completely avoid cross-contamination.
- **Structure Impermeability**: Floors must be non-slippery and sloped toward drains with grease traps; walls and ceilings must be free from flaking paint, plaster, and condensation.
- **Separate Zones**: Dedicated, isolated processing zones must exist for heat-treated milk versus raw handling to prevent post-pasteurization contamination.

## Critical Operation & Process Controls
- **Raw Material Inspection**: Raw milk must be evaluated at receipt for organoleptic properties, temperature, and basic adulteration parameters (e.g., MBRT, clot-on-boiling, alcohol test).
- **Temperature Management**: Cold chain maintenance is critical. Raw milk must be chilled immediately to 4°C or below and stored at compliant temperatures.
- **HACCP & FSMS Implementation**: A robust Food Safety Management System (FSMS) plan mapping out Critical Control Points (CCPs)—such as pasteurization temperature/time configuration (72°C for 15 seconds)—is required.
- **Stock Rotation**: Inventory management must strictly follow FIFO (First In, First Out) or FEFO (First Expired, First Out) practices.

## Maintenance, Sanitation & Waste Management
- **Food-Grade Equipment**: All product-contact surfaces, including silos, pasteurizers, and tankers, must be made of non-corrosive, food-grade materials like Stainless Steel 304/316.
- **Effluent Treatment Plant (ETP)**: An operational ETP must be available on-site, complying entirely with local Environment Protection Act standards.
- **CIP (Clean-in-Place)**: Verification logs for daily CIP cycles of milk pipelines, storage tanks, and pasteurizers must be actively recorded.

## Documentation, Sampling & Traceability
- **Mandatory Semi-Annual Testing**: Milk products must be tested for chemical and microbiological contaminants through an internal or NABL-accredited/FSSAI-notified lab at least once every six months.
- **Recall Plan**: A documented Food Recall Procedure along with proof of a mock recall executed within the trailing 12 months is mandatory.
- **Record Retention**: General manufacturing logs, batch processing details, and pasteurization charts must be systematically archived for at least one year or the shelf-life of the product, whichever is longer.
