# FSSAI Schedule 4 — Part III: Food Safety Requirements for Street Food Vendors and Small Food Businesses

**Source Body:** FSSAI  
**Document:** Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule 4, Part III  
**Amendment Date:** 2023-08-01  
**Product Categories:** street_food, catering  
**Hazard Types:** biological, chemical, physical  

---

## 1. Scope and Applicability

Part III covers all food business operators involved in:

- Street vending of cooked, semi-processed, or packaged foods (thela, dhabas, carts, mobile units)
- Temporary food stalls at fairs, festivals, and public events
- Small-scale food service businesses with annual turnover below ₹12 lakhs (registration threshold)

Street food vendors must at minimum be registered under the FSS Act 2006. Vendors near schools, hospitals, and public institutions face enhanced compliance requirements.

---

## 2. Personal Hygiene Requirements — Critical

### 2.1 Food Handler Hygiene

- **CRITICAL (*):** Food handlers must NOT handle food if suffering from jaundice, diarrhoea, vomiting, fever, sore throat with fever, visibly infected skin lesions, or discharge from eye/ear/nose
- Handwashing: food handlers must wash hands with soap and clean water before handling food, after handling raw meat or poultry, after toilet use, after handling garbage, after coughing or sneezing
- Fingernails: short, clean, no nail polish
- Hair: properly covered with cap, net, or headscarf at all times during food handling
- Jewellery: not permitted on wrists, hands, or fingers (risk of physical contamination)
- Apron/uniform: clean, light-coloured, changed daily
- No smoking, eating, or chewing tobacco/pan while handling food

### 2.2 Medical Fitness

- All food handlers must have a medical fitness certificate from a registered medical practitioner — renewed annually
- Typhoid vaccination recommended; hepatitis A vaccination encouraged

## 3. Food Handling and Preparation — Critical Control Points

### 3.1 CCP 1 — Cooking/Heat Treatment

- **Critical Limit:** All cooked foods must reach internal temperature ≥75°C
  - For poultry: ≥80°C at the thickest point
  - For minced meat products: ≥72°C throughout
  - For rice, dals, curries: ≥75°C at service
- **Monitoring:** Use of food thermometer at service point (or time-temperature equivalent)
- **Corrective Action:** Return food to heat source; do not serve undercooked food

### 3.2 CCP 2 — Hot-Holding Temperature

- **Critical Limit:** Cooked food held hot must be maintained at ≥60°C at all times
- Cold foods (chaat, raita, salads): must be held at ≤10°C or consumed within 2 hours of preparation
- **Monitoring:** Temperature check every 1 hour
- **Corrective Action:** Reheat to ≥75°C if temperature falls below 60°C; discard if held below 60°C for >2 hours

### 3.3 CCP 3 — Water Quality

- **Critical Limit:** Only potable water (municipality supplied or treated source) permitted for cooking, washing equipment/utensils, and washing produce
- Water storage containers must be food-grade, clean, and covered
- Ice used in beverages or food contact: must be made from potable water
- **Monitoring:** Monthly water quality certificate from certified laboratory (E. coli absent in 100 mL)
- **Corrective Action:** Stop using water source; switch to bottled/treated water; report to municipal authority

### 3.4 CCP 4 — Cross-Contamination Prevention

- Raw meat, poultry, fish, and eggs must be stored separately from ready-to-eat foods
- Separate cutting boards and utensils for raw vs. cooked foods (colour-coded preferred)
- No raw food preparation on surfaces used for serving
- **CRITICAL (*):** Street food vendors must never use the same utensils for raw and cooked food without washing and sanitising between uses

## 4. Biological Hazards for Street Food

### 4.1 Key Pathogens

| Pathogen | Associated Street Foods | Preventive Control |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Salmonella Typhi | Chaat, fruit salad, juices | Water safety, personal hygiene |
| Vibrio cholerae | Seafood, water-based beverages | Potable water, thorough cooking |
| Staphylococcus aureus | Rice dishes, pastries, chaat | Personal hygiene, temperature control |
| E. coli (ETEC) | Uncooked salads, juices, water | Water quality, handwashing |
| Norovirus | Ready-to-eat items | Personal hygiene, clean water |
| Bacillus cereus | Rice, dals, khichdi | Temperature control (hot-hold >60°C) |
| Hepatitis A | Raw vegetables, juices | Water safety, vaccination |

### 4.2 Hazard Risk Matrix (Street Food)

| Process Step | Hazard | Likelihood | Severity | RPN | CCP? |
|-------------|--------|-----------|---------|-----|------|
| Water usage | Waterborne pathogens | 4 | 5 | 20 | CCP 3 |
| Cooking | Survival of pathogens | 4 | 5 | 20 | CCP 1 |
| Hot-holding | Pathogen growth | 4 | 4 | 16 | CCP 2 |
| Cross-contamination | Salmonella, E. coli | 4 | 4 | 16 | CCP 4 |
| Food handler | Staphylococcus, Norovirus | 3 | 4 | 12 | Control point |

## 5. Chemical Hazard Considerations

### 5.1 Unapproved Food Additives

- **CRITICAL (*):** Use of prohibited synthetic colours (Sudan dyes, metanil yellow, auramine) in street food is a serious violation — leads to license cancellation
- Permitted colouring agents: only FSSAIs positive list (Annex to FSS Regulations 2011, as amended)
- Preservatives: common street food should not require preservatives; fermented preparations may use approved amounts

### 5.2 Adulterants

- Common adulterants in street food ingredients:
  - Khoya adulteration with starch/vegetable fat
  - Paneer adulteration with starch/skim milk powder
  - Oil reuse: edible oils reused more than 3 times — Total Polar Compounds (TPC) ≥ 25% indicates deterioration — must discard
- **CRITICAL (*):** Reuse of oil exceeding TPC of 25% is prohibited under FSSAI (oil monitoring regulations 2023)

### 5.3 Pesticide Residues

- Fruits and vegetables used as toppings/garnishes must be washed with clean water; avoid use of produce with visible pesticide residues or pest damage

---

## 6. Equipment and Premises Requirements

- Cooking equipment must be in good working order, clean, and maintained on a non-combustible surface
- Display cases must be covered and protected from flies, dust, and contamination
- Single-use plates and cups recommended; if reusable, must be washed with detergent and rinsed with potable water between uses
- Waste disposal: covered waste bin present; waste removed regularly (not allowed to accumulate)
- No bare-hand contact with ready-to-eat food: use tongs, ladles, or disposable gloves

---

## 7. Display and Labelling

- Pre-packaged items sold by street vendors must carry legible labels with: name of food, ingredient list, name and address of manufacturer, net quantity, date of manufacture, best before date, batch number, FSSAI license number
- Self-prepared items: vendor's FSSAI registration number must be displayed prominently at the stall

---

## 8. Record Keeping (Minimum for Street Vendors)

- Copy of FSSAI registration certificate displayed at stall
- Medical fitness certificates for all food handlers (renewed annually)
- Potable water test certificate (if using non-municipal water source)
- Pest control records (if pest control service used)
