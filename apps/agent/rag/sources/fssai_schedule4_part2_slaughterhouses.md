# FSSAI Schedule 4 — Part II: Requirements for Slaughterhouses, Meat and Poultry Processing

**Source Body:** FSSAI  
**Document:** Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule 4, Part II  
**Amendment Date:** 2024-01-15  
**Product Categories:** meat, seafood  
**Hazard Types:** biological, chemical, physical  

---

## 1. Scope and Applicability

Part II of Schedule 4 applies to all food business operators (FBOs) engaged in:

- Slaughter of food animals (cattle, buffalo, sheep, goat, pig, poultry)
- Processing of meat, poultry, and meat products
- Meat packaging, storage, and distribution
- Rendering operations

All slaughterhouses and meat processing units must be licensed under the FSS Act 2006 and comply with all provisions of this Part.

---

## 2. Infrastructure Requirements

### 2.1 Ante-Mortem Inspection Area

- Dedicated holding pens with adequate space (minimum 1.8 sq m per large animal, 0.5 sq m per small animal)
- Potable water supply for cleaning at minimum 20 litres per large animal per day
- Adequate drainage and waste management
- Separate areas for sick/suspect animals — must be marked and physically separated
- Lighting: minimum 500 lux in inspection area

### 2.2 Slaughter Hall

- *Construction requirements:* Walls and floors of impervious, washable material (ceramic tiles or equivalent)
- Drainage: sloped floors (minimum 2% gradient) draining to covered floor drains
- Overhead rails for carcass handling — minimum clearance 3.5 m for large animals
- Separation between dirty and clean zones (ante-mortem vs. post-mortem processing)
- HVAC: Air exchange minimum 20 changes per hour in slaughter hall

### 2.3 Cold Storage Requirements

- Pre-chilling room: Temperature 0–7°C, maintained continuously
- Freezer room: Temperature −18°C or below for frozen meat
- Carcass must reach core temperature of 7°C within 24 hours of slaughter (beef/buffalo), 4°C within 4 hours (poultry)
- Separate chillers for different species to prevent cross-contamination

---

## 3. Ante-Mortem and Post-Mortem Inspection — Critical Control Points

### 3.1 Ante-Mortem Inspection

- All animals must be inspected by a licensed Veterinary Officer (VO) within 24 hours before slaughter
- Animals showing signs of disease must be segregated immediately — non-conforming animals are not permitted to enter the slaughter hall
- Records of ante-mortem inspection must be maintained (species, age, owner, condition, disposition)
- **CRITICAL REQUIREMENT (*):** Any animal with signs of foot-and-mouth disease, anthrax, rabies, tuberculosis, brucellosis, or African swine fever must be reported to the district veterinary authority immediately and the animal condemned

### 3.2 Post-Mortem Inspection

- All carcasses must undergo post-mortem inspection before release for consumption
- Inspecting areas: head, viscera (lungs, heart, liver, spleen, kidneys), carcass
- Carcass stamping: All passed carcasses must be marked with a food-grade stamp bearing the plant's FSSAI license number
- **CRITICAL REQUIREMENT (*):** Carcasses showing evidence of generalised disease (tuberculosis, brucellosis, CLA, abscesses involving lymph nodes), severe emaciation, or signs of contamination with ingesta, faeces, or bile must be condemned in their entirety

## 4. Biological Hazard Analysis — Meat Processing

### 4.1 Key Biological Hazards

| Hazard | Source | Critical Limit | Severity |
|--------|--------|----------------|----------|
| Salmonella spp. | Animal intestinal flora | Absence in 25 g test sample | High (5) |
| E. coli O157:H7 | Cattle faecal contamination | Absence in 25 g test sample | Critical (5) |
| Listeria monocytogenes | Environment, cross-contamination | Absence in 25 g (RTE) / <100 CFU/g (raw) | High (5) |
| Campylobacter jejuni | Poultry intestinal flora | <100 CFU/g (carcass rinse) | High (4) |
| Staphylococcus aureus | Handler contamination | <100 CFU/g | Medium (3) |
| Clostridium perfringens | Spore-forming — cooking/cooling CCP | <10 CFU/g (cooked products) | Medium (3) |
| Taenia saginata/solium | Cattle/pig muscle (Cysticercus) | Absence on inspection | High (4) |
| Trichinella spiralis | Pork muscle | Absence on inspection/freezing | High (4) |

### 4.2 Critical Control Points in Meat Processing

**CCP 1 — Ante-Mortem Inspection**
- Parameter: Animal health status
- Critical Limit: No animals with notifiable diseases enter slaughter floor
- Monitoring: 100% inspection by licensed VO
- Corrective Action: Condemn animal, notify authorities

**CCP 2 — Evisceration (Contamination Prevention)**
- Parameter: Absence of faecal/ingesta contamination of carcass surface
- Critical Limit: Zero visible contamination
- Monitoring: Visual inspection by QA at each carcass
- Corrective Action: Decontaminate with 82°C water wash or trim contaminated area

**CCP 3 — Chilling**
- Parameter: Carcass core temperature
- Critical Limit: ≤7°C within 24 hours of slaughter (red meat); ≤4°C within 4 hours (poultry)
- Monitoring: Temperature probe check every 4 hours during chilling
- Corrective Action: Extend chilling time; if temperature not achieved within tolerance window, divert for cooking or condemn

**CCP 4 — Cooking (for cooked meat products)**
- Parameter: Internal temperature of product
- Critical Limit: ≥72°C for 15 seconds (poultry); ≥71°C for 15 seconds (beef, pork)
- Monitoring: Calibrated probe thermometer at thickest point of product
- Corrective Action: Re-cook to required temperature; if equipment failure, hold and quarantine batch

**CCP 5 — Freezing (for frozen products)**
- Parameter: Core temperature
- Critical Limit: ≤−18°C within 24 hours of packaging
- Monitoring: Temperature logger at geometric centre of largest unit
- Corrective Action: Extend freezing time; do not dispatch product that has not reached −18°C

## 5. Chemical Hazard Requirements

### 5.1 Veterinary Drug Residues

- Withdrawal periods for antibiotics, NSAIDs, antiparasitics, and corticosteroids must be observed per label instructions
- FSSAI maximum residue limits (MRLs) apply: e.g., Oxytetracycline ≤ 100 μg/kg (muscle), Chloramphenicol: absent (zero tolerance)
- Testing frequency: minimum 1% of production lots randomly tested per FSSAI surveillance plan

### 5.2 Cleaning Chemical Contamination

- Cleaning and disinfection must be performed with food-grade approved chemicals only
- Pre-operational sanitation verification: ATP bioluminescence swab test — surface <50 RLU (relative light units)
- No residual quaternary ammonium compounds, chlorine, or peracetic acid on product contact surfaces before production restart

### 5.3 Heavy Metals

- Lead ≤ 0.1 mg/kg (muscle tissue)
- Cadmium ≤ 0.05 mg/kg (muscle); ≤ 1.0 mg/kg (kidney)
- Mercury ≤ 0.02 mg/kg

---

## 6. Physical Hazard Controls

- Metal detection: mandatory for all packaged meat products. Sensitivity: ≥1.5 mm ferrous, ≥2.0 mm non-ferrous, ≥2.5 mm stainless steel
- Bone control: X-ray or manual inspection for mechanically separated meat (MSM) — reject if bone particles >3 mm detected
- Equipment integrity checks: all blades, saws, and tools inventoried daily; any missing piece triggers product hold until fragment is located

## 8. Record Keeping Requirements

- Ante-mortem inspection records: species, head count, VO signature, disposition — retained 3 years
- Post-mortem condemnation records: animal ID, reason for condemnation — retained 3 years
- Chilling temperature logs: continuous digital records retained 12 months
- Cleaning and disinfection logs: product, concentration, method, staff ID — retained 12 months
- Veterinary drug withdrawal certificates from farmers — retained 2 years
- Metal detector calibration and test records — retained 12 months
- Microbiological test certificates — retained 12 months
