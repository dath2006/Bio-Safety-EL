# FSSAI Schedule 4 — Part VI: Requirements for Catering Establishments and Food Service Operations

**Source Body:** FSSAI  
**Document:** Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule 4, Part VI  
**Amendment Date:** 2024-01-01  
**Product Categories:** catering, rte  
**Hazard Types:** biological, chemical, physical  

---

## 1. Scope

Part VI applies to:

- Restaurants, dhabas, canteens, hotels (food service component)
- Industrial and institutional canteens (hospital, school, college, government)
- Airline catering facilities
- Banquet and event catering
- Cloud kitchens and online food delivery operations

---

## 2. Infrastructure and Kitchen Requirements

### 2.1 Kitchen Design Principles

- **Unidirectional flow:** Raw materials enter from one side; finished products exit from the opposite — prevents cross-contamination
- **Separate zones:**
  - Raw preparation area (vegetables, meat, fish)
  - Cooking area
  - Cold food preparation area (chilled, RTE salads, desserts)
  - Plating and service area
  - Cleaning and dishwashing area
  - Dry storage and refrigeration

### 2.2 Ventilation

- Exhaust hoods over all cooking equipment — minimum 0.3 m canopy beyond cooking equipment perimeter
- Kitchen air exchange: minimum 15 changes per hour
- All grease traps cleaned and inspected quarterly

### 2.3 Surface Standards

- All food contact surfaces: stainless steel grade 304 minimum, or food-grade approved material
- No wooden cutting boards for meat or ready-to-eat preparations (risk of harbouring bacteria in grain)
- Colour-coded cutting boards: red (raw meat), blue (seafood), yellow (poultry), green (vegetables/fruit), white (cooked/RTE)

## 3. Critical Control Points for Catering Operations

### 3.1 CCP 1 — Cooking / Heat Treatment

- **Critical Limit:** Internal temperature ≥75°C for all hot cooked foods (except where specific limits apply)
- Poultry: ≥80°C at thickest point
- Stuffed items: ≥75°C at geometric centre
- **CRITICAL (*):** No food should leave the kitchen for service below the following temperatures:
  - Hot food: ≥60°C
  - Cold food: ≤10°C
- Monitoring: Temperature probe check on representative portion per batch
- Corrective Action: Return to heat; if repeat failure, investigate equipment

### 3.2 CCP 2 — Hot-Holding at Service

- **Critical Limit:** ≥60°C throughout the service period
- Chafing dishes, bain-maries, heated trolleys: must maintain ≥60°C
- Time limit for hot-holding: maximum 4 hours; after 4 hours, discard if not consumed
- **CRITICAL (*):** Food may NOT be re-heated more than once. Repeatedly heated food must be discarded after service
- Monitoring: Temperature check every 30 minutes during service
- Corrective Action: If temperature drops below 60°C, reheat immediately to ≥75°C once; if temperature not recoverable, discard

### 3.3 CCP 3 — Cold-Holding and Refrigeration

- **Critical Limit:** ≤4°C for chilled food preparation and service
- Cold food at buffet service: maximum 2 hours at ambient temperature; after 2 hours, discard or return to refrigeration within 30 minutes
- Refrigerator temperature monitoring: checked and logged twice daily (morning before service and close of business)

### 3.4 CCP 4 — Blast Chilling of Cooked-Chill Products

- Applicable to cook-chill systems (large institutions, airline catering)
- **Critical Limit:** Cooked products must cool from 65°C to ≤10°C within 2 hours in a blast chiller
- Blast chilled products stored at ≤3°C; shelf life: maximum 5 days
- Before service: reheat to ≥75°C core temperature

## 4. Food Handler Requirements

### 4.1 Health and Hygiene

- **CRITICAL (*):** Food handlers with diarrhoea, vomiting, jaundice, fever, infected wounds, or respiratory infection must be excluded from food handling until medically cleared
- Annual medical fitness certificate for all food handlers
- Typhoid vaccination strongly recommended; hepatitis A vaccination encouraged
- Handwashing: before starting work, after handling raw food, after toilet visits, after handling waste, after coughing/sneezing

### 4.2 Training Requirements

- All food handlers must have undergone basic food hygiene training (minimum 4 hours per FSSAI food safety training guidelines)
- FoSTaC (Food Safety Training and Certification) — FSSAI's official training programme — required for supervisors and food safety managers
- Training records retained for all employees

---

## 5. Delivery Operations (Cloud Kitchens / Online Food Delivery)

Additional requirements for food delivered to consumers:

- **Hot food delivery:** Food must be packaged in insulated containers that maintain food ≥60°C at point of delivery; if delivery time >45 minutes, supplementary heating mechanism required
- **Cold food delivery:** Must maintain ≤10°C throughout delivery; if delivery time >30 minutes without refrigeration, products must be pre-chilled to ≤2°C before packaging
- Packaging integrity: tamper-evident seals on all delivered food containers
- **FSSAI Online Food Aggregator Guidelines 2020:** All cloud kitchens must display FSSAI license number on the food delivery app listing

---

## 6. Allergen Management in Catering

- Menu engineering: flag allergen-containing dishes on the menu (minimum 14 allergens per FSSAI guidelines)
- On-request disclosure: staff trained to provide full ingredient list on customer request
- Cross-contact prevention: separate utensils for allergen-free dishes; or clean and sanitise between preparations
- **CRITICAL (*):** Failure to disclose allergen-containing ingredients that causes anaphylaxis is subject to prosecution under FSS Act 2006

## 8. Pest Control Requirements

- Written pest management programme with a licensed pest control operator (PCO)
- Pest control records: date, time, pests targeted, chemicals used, areas treated — retained 12 months
- **CRITICAL (*):** Evidence of active rodent infestation (live or dead rodents, droppings in food storage) is an immediate closure trigger
- Fly control: UV fly-o-cuters in all food preparation areas, checked and cleaned weekly
- All external openings screened with 1.5 mm mesh

---

## 9. Cleaning and Disinfection

- Master cleaning schedule: specifies what is cleaned, how, when, by whom
- Food contact surfaces cleaned and sanitised after every use (minimum) or every 4 hours during continuous operations
- Sanitiser concentration validation: chlorine-based 100–200 ppm active chlorine; quaternary ammonium compounds at label concentration
- Dishwasher validation: minimum rinse temperature ≥82°C (hot sanitation) or validated chemical sanitation cycle
