# FSSAI Schedule 4 — Part V: Food Safety Requirements for Cold Chain, Storage, and Transportation

**Source Body:** FSSAI  
**Document:** Food Safety and Standards (Licensing and Registration of Food Businesses) Regulations, 2011 — Schedule 4, Part V  
**Amendment Date:** 2024-03-01  
**Product Categories:** cold_chain, dairy, meat, seafood, rte  
**Hazard Types:** biological, chemical, physical  

---

## 1. Scope and Applicability

Part V covers FBOs engaged in:

- Cold storage operations (public and private)
- Refrigerated transport and logistics (including reefer vehicles)
- Warehousing of food products requiring temperature control
- Frozen food distribution chains

---

## 2. Temperature Requirements — Core Critical Control Points

### 2.1 Product Category Temperature Requirements

| Product Type | Storage Temperature | Transport Temperature |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Fresh vegetables and fruits | 0–10°C | ≤10°C |
| Fresh dairy (milk, paneer, yoghurt) | ≤4°C | ≤4°C |
| Fresh meat and poultry | 0–4°C | ≤4°C |
| Fresh fish and seafood | 0–4°C (ice) | ≤4°C |
| Cooked/RTE chilled food | ≤4°C | ≤4°C |
| Frozen meat/poultry/seafood | ≤−18°C | ≤−18°C |
| Frozen dairy/ice cream | ≤−18°C | ≤−18°C |
| Frozen ready-to-eat meals | ≤−18°C | ≤−18°C |

### 2.2 Temperature Excursion Limits

- **CRITICAL (*):** Products that have breached temperature limits and cannot be confirmed to have maintained safety must be quarantined and assessed by a food safety professional before release
- For dairy: milk that has been above 8°C for >4 hours must be rejected
- For frozen products: partially thawed and refrozen products are prohibited for resale

---

## 3. Cold Storage Facility Requirements

### 3.1 Infrastructure

- Insulated panels: minimum insulation R-value meeting the design capacity and ambient temperature range
- Refrigeration capacity: verified to maintain design temperature even when loading/unloading doors are open for normal operational periods
- Temperature monitoring: calibrated temperature data loggers at minimum 2 positions per zone (warmest and coldest points), with data retention ≥6 months
- Alarm system: automatic high-temperature alarm set at 2°C above critical limit, with audible and/or remote notification

### 3.2 Defrost Cycles

- Defrost cycles must not raise product temperature above the critical storage temperature for the product type
- Defrost time-temperature records must be maintained
- Products should be temporarily relocated or covered during defrost if temperature is at risk

### 3.3 Cold Storage Door Management

- Air curtains or PVC strip curtains required at all cold room entrances
- Door-open alarms required for doors left open >5 minutes
- Pre-cooling of new product deliveries before entry into storage (staging area temperature ≤10°C)

---

## 4. Refrigerated Transport Requirements

### 4.1 Vehicle Standards

- **CRITICAL (*):** Reefer vehicles used for distributing temperature-controlled foods must be pre-cooled to required delivery temperature before loading
- Vehicle interior must be insulated and free from cracks, rust, and contamination
- Separate compartments or physical separation required for raw and cooked food during transport
- No transport of non-food items (chemicals, petroleum products) in the same compartment as food

### 4.2 Temperature Monitoring in Transport

- Continuous temperature recording device (data logger or chart recorder) installed in each reefer vehicle
- Driver must check and record temperature at start of journey, every 2 hours during transit, and on delivery
- Deviation records: if temperature excursion detected, consignment is quarantined at delivery point pending risk assessment

### 4.3 Loading and Unloading

- Loading and unloading must be done as quickly as possible to minimise temperature exposure
- Docking/loading bays must be covered and controlled for temperature where possible
- First-in-first-out (FIFO) stock rotation must be implemented

## 5. Biological Hazards in Cold Chain Operations

### 5.1 Key Psychrotrophic Pathogens

| Pathogen | Growth at <4°C | Critical Limit | Product at Risk |
|---------|--------------|---------------|----------------|
| Listeria monocytogenes | Yes (slow, down to −0.4°C) | Absence in 25 g (RTE) | RTE chilled foods, dairy |
| Yersinia enterocolitica | Yes | <100 CFU/g | Pork, raw milk |
| Aeromonas hydrophila | Yes | <10³ CFU/g | Fish, seafood |
| Pseudomonas spp. | Yes | Spoilage indicator | Meat, dairy |

### 5.2 Biofilm Risk in Cold Rooms

- Regular sanitisation of cold room surfaces, racks, drains, and drain covers
- Listeria environmental monitoring programme: quarterly swabs from floor/wall junctions, drain covers, pallet tracks
- **CRITICAL (*):** Positive Listeria environmental test in cold room requires immediate deep sanitation, re-testing before resuming operations, and documentation

---

## 6. Chemical Hazards in Cold Chain

- Refrigerants: leakage of refrigerants (ammonia, Freon) must be monitored; ammonia leak detectors mandatory in ammonia-based refrigeration systems
- Cleaning agents: cleaning of cold rooms must be done with food-grade sanitisers; no residual chemical on product contact surfaces
- Pest control bait stations: must be tamper-resistant and placed only in non-product areas (outside cold storage)

---

## 7. Stock Management and FIFO

- Date coding: all stored products must have visible date codes (manufacturing date, expiry date)
- FIFO rotation enforced: newest stock placed behind existing stock
- Damaged goods: damaged, leaking, or mislabelled product segregated in a dedicated quarantine area
- Stock rotation records maintained for all temperature-controlled products

---

## 8. Record Keeping Requirements

- Continuous temperature logs from all data loggers — minimum 6-month retention
- Vehicle temperature records for each delivery run
- Sanitation records for cold room cleaning — minimum 12-month retention
- Calibration certificates for all temperature measuring equipment (annual calibration minimum)
- Stock in/out records with lot numbers for traceability
- Defrost cycle records
- Listeria environmental monitoring results
