# FSSAI Product Standards for Ready-to-Eat (RTE) Meals and Processed Food Products

**Source Body:** FSSAI  
**Document:** Food Safety and Standards (Food Products Standards and Food Additives) Regulations, 2011 — RTE and Processed Food Standards; FSSAI Notification 2023 on RTE Food Safety  
**Amendment Date:** 2023-11-15  
**Product Categories:** rte, catering  
**Hazard Types:** biological, chemical, physical  

---

## 1. Definition and Scope

A **Ready-to-Eat (RTE) food** is defined under FSSAI as: *"food intended for direct human consumption without further cooking or heat treatment by the consumer."* This includes:

- Pre-cooked chilled meals (rice dishes, curries, dals, rotis with accompaniments)
- Pre-cooked frozen meals
- Salads, sandwiches, and wraps
- Pre-cooked snacks (samosas, kachori, spring rolls — heat-and-serve or eat-as-is)
- Packaged ready-to-eat ethnic dishes
- Hospital meal trays and airline catering

## 2. Microbiological Standards for RTE Food

### 2.1 Core Microbiological Criteria (FSSAI, as amended 2023)

| Organism | Acceptable Limit | Test Method |
|---------|-----------------|------------|
| Total Plate Count (TPC) | ≤10⁵ CFU/g | IS 5402:2012 |
| Coliform count | ≤10² CFU/g | IS 5401 (Pt 1):2012 |
| Faecal Coliforms (E. coli) | Absent in 1 g | IS 5401 (Pt 1):2012 |
| Salmonella spp. | Absent in 25 g | IS 5887 (Pt 1):2012 |
| Staphylococcus aureus | ≤10² CFU/g | IS 5887 (Pt 2):2012 |
| Listeria monocytogenes | Absent in 25 g | ISO 11290-1:2017 |
| Bacillus cereus | ≤10³ CFU/g | IS 5887 (Pt 6):2012 |
| Clostridium perfringens | ≤10² CFU/g | ISO 7937:2004 |
| Yeast and Mould | ≤10³ CFU/g | IS 5403:2012 |

**Non-conformance action:** Any RTE product failing the above criteria must be withdrawn from sale, quarantined, and destroyed or reworked (if rework makes it no longer RTE).

## 3. Critical Control Points for RTE Meal Production

### 3.1 CCP 1 — Cooking/Heat Treatment

- **Parameter:** Internal product temperature
- **Critical Limit:** ≥75°C for ≥15 seconds (rice, dals, curries, sauces); ≥80°C (poultry components)
- **Monitoring:** Digital probe thermometer, each batch, at thickest point
- **Corrective Action:** Re-cook; do not pack non-conforming lots; investigate root cause

### 3.2 CCP 2 — Post-Cook Rapid Cooling (Chilled RTE)

- **Parameter:** Core temperature reduction rate
- **Critical Limit:** Product must cool from 75°C to ≤10°C within 2 hours using blast chilling
- Blast chiller set point: ≤0°C (blast), or ≤−30°C (for shock freezing of frozen RTE)
- **CRITICAL (*):** Slow cooling (natural ambient cooling) of bulk cooked RTE products is not permitted — this is a leading cause of Bacillus cereus and Clostridium perfringens outbreaks
- **Monitoring:** Temperature data logger inserted into largest portion size during blast chilling cycle
- **Corrective Action:** If cooling target not achieved in 2 hours — discard, do not reuse product; clean and check blast chiller capacity

### 3.3 CCP 3 — Packaging Under Controlled Environment

- RTE products must be portioned and packaged in temperature-controlled area (≤10°C ambient for chilled products)
- Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP): if used, gas mixture (N₂/CO₂) must be validated for the specific product and shelf life claim
- Packaging area hygiene: follows clean room principles — positive air pressure, HEPA filtration if applicable, restricted entry

### 3.4 CCP 4 — Cold Storage of Finished Product

- **Critical Limit:** Storage ≤4°C for chilled RTE; ≤−18°C for frozen RTE
- Shelf life: chilled RTE maximum 5 days at ≤4°C (unless validated for longer); frozen RTE as per manufacturer's validated shelf life
- FIFO rotation mandatory

## 4. Labelling Requirements for RTE Products

All packaged RTE products must include (FSSAI Food Safety Labelling Regulations):

- Product name ("Ready to Eat" or "Ready to Heat and Eat" — distinction required)
- Ingredient list in descending order by weight
- Allergen declaration: dairy, gluten, eggs, nuts, soy, mustard clearly highlighted
- Net weight
- Name and address of manufacturer with FSSAI license number
- Batch/lot number
- Date of manufacture
- Best before / use-by date
- Storage instructions (e.g., "Keep refrigerated at ≤4°C")
- Reheating instructions (if applicable): "Heat to ≥75°C before serving"
- Country of origin (for imports)

## 5. Allergen Management in RTE Production

- **CRITICAL (*):** Undeclared allergens in RTE food is a Class I recall trigger under FSSAI
- Allergen control plan mandatory: identify all allergens in recipe, manage ingredient procurement, prevent cross-contact during production
- Physical separation: separate allergen-containing ingredients; dedicated equipment or verified cleaning between production runs
- Label verification: pre-shift label check to confirm correct label applied to correct product

Common allergens in Indian RTE:
- Gluten (wheat in roti, paratha, samosa crust)
- Milk (paneer, ghee, curd in most curries)
- Peanuts (commonly used as cooking medium)
- Mustard seeds (tadka, pickles)
- Sesame (til in chikki, garnishes)
- Tree nuts (cashew, almond in gravies, desserts)

## 6. Shelf Life Validation Requirements

- Microbiological shelf life study must be conducted for each product claim
- Challenge testing for Listeria monocytogenes required for chilled RTE products with shelf life >5 days
- Chemical shelf life markers: pH, Aw (water activity), oxidation (peroxide value for fat-containing products)
- Minimum sampling plan for shelf life validation: n=5 at day 0, n=3 at mid-shelf life, n=5 at end of shelf life

| Norovirus | Handler contamination | 3 | 4 | 12 | Sick worker exclusion, hygiene |

## 8. Environmental Monitoring Programme

For RTE production areas, an Environmental Monitoring Programme (EMP) is mandatory:

- Listeria spp. (indicator organism) environmental swabs: minimum 10 sites/week during production
- Listeria monocytogenes confirmation testing: any positive indicator sample triggers immediate corrective action
- Zone 1 (direct product contact surfaces): highest frequency monitoring
- Zone 2 (equipment near product): weekly
- Zone 3 (non-product contact surfaces): monthly
- Zone 4 (external environment, drains, floors): quarterly

---

## 9. Record Keeping for RTE Operations

- Cook temperature logs: every batch — retained 12 months
- Blast chilling temperature logs: every batch — retained 12 months
- Finished product microbiological release certificates: every lot — retained 12 months after expiry
- Environmental monitoring swab results: retained 2 years
- Allergen cleaning verification records: every changeover — retained 12 months
- Shelf life validation study reports: retained for 2 years beyond product discontinuation
- Customer complaint records and investigation reports: retained 3 years
