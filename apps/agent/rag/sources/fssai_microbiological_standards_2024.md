# FSSAI Microbiological Standards for Food Products (2024)

**Source:** Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI)
**Regulation:** Food Safety and Standards (Food Products Standards and Food Additives) Regulations — Schedule III: Microbiological Standards
**Amendment Date:** 2024-03-01
**Applicable Categories:** dairy, dairy_pasteurized, meat, seafood, rte, beverages, packaged_food, general

---

## 1. Introduction

These microbiological standards specify the **maximum permissible levels** of pathogenic and indicator microorganisms in food products sold in India. Compliance with these standards is mandatory for all FBOs. Non-compliance constitutes an unsafe food product under Section 3(1)(zz) of the Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006.

## 2. Dairy Products Microbiological Standards

### 2.1 Pasteurized Cow/Buffalo Milk

| Microorganism | Critical Limit | Sampling Method |
|---|---|---|
| Total Plate Count (TPC) | ≤ 50,000 CFU/mL | ISO 4833 |
| Coliform count | ≤ 10 CFU/mL | ISO 4831 |
| Yeast and Mould | ≤ 10 CFU/mL | ISO 6611 |
| Staphylococcus aureus | Absent in 1 mL | ISO 6888 |
| Salmonella spp. | Absent in 25 mL | ISO 6579 |
| Listeria monocytogenes | Absent in 25 mL | ISO 11290 |

**HACCP Note:** Pasteurization is the primary CCP for dairy products. Time-temperature must achieve ≥ 5-log reduction of L. monocytogenes. Standard: 72°C for ≥ 15 seconds (HTST) or 63°C for ≥ 30 minutes (LTLT).

### 2.2 Ultra-High Temperature (UHT) Treated Milk

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Commercial sterility | Achieved — no growth at 37°C for 7 days |
| Thermophilic spore-forming bacteria | ≤ 100 CFU/500 mL |

### 2.3 Paneer (Fresh Cheese)

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Total Plate Count | ≤ 1,000,000 CFU/g (10⁶) |
| Coliform count | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| Yeast and Mould | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| Staphylococcus aureus (coagulase positive) | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| Salmonella spp. | Absent in 25 g |
| Listeria monocytogenes | Absent in 25 g |
| E. coli (STEC O157:H7) | Absent in 25 g |

### 2.4 Yoghurt, Dahi, and Fermented Dairy Products

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Total Plate Count (non-starter bacteria) | ≤ 100,000 CFU/g |
| Yeast and Mould | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| Coliform | ≤ 10 CFU/g |
| Salmonella | Absent in 25 g |
| Staphylococcus aureus | ≤ 10 CFU/g |
| Lactic acid bacteria (minimum, for labelled benefit) | ≥ 10⁶ CFU/g at end of shelf life |

### 2.5 Butter and Ghee

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Total Plate Count | ≤ 500 CFU/g |
| Yeast and Mould | ≤ 10 CFU/g |
| Coliform | Absent in 1 g |
| Salmonella | Absent in 25 g |

## 3. Meat and Poultry Microbiological Standards

### 3.1 Fresh Chicken (Raw)

| Microorganism | Critical Limit | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| Total Aerobic Count | ≤ 10⁶ CFU/g | At retail |
| Salmonella spp. | ≤ 1 positive/25 samples | Per lot |
| Campylobacter spp. | ≤ 1 positive/25 samples | Per lot |
| Staphylococcus aureus | ≤ 1,000 CFU/g | At retail |
| E. coli generic | ≤ 100 CFU/g | At retail |

**HACCP Note:** For raw poultry, the CCP is cooking. The thermal kill step (≥ 74°C internal for poultry) controls Salmonella and Campylobacter. Pre-cooking hygiene is PRP.

### 3.2 Cooked/RTE Meat Products (Ham, Sausage, Cooked Chicken)

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Listeria monocytogenes | Absent in 25 g (zero tolerance) |
| Salmonella spp. | Absent in 25 g |
| Staphylococcus aureus (coagulase positive) | ≤ 100 CFU/g |
| Total Plate Count | ≤ 100,000 CFU/g |
| E. coli | Absent in 1 g |
| Clostridium perfringens | ≤ 100 CFU/g |

**HACCP Note:** L. monocytogenes has zero tolerance in RTE meat because these products receive no further cooking. Post-process contamination control is CCP.

### 3.3 Minced Meat / Ground Beef

| Microorganism | Critical Limit |
|---|---|
| Total Aerobic Count | ≤ 5 × 10⁶ CFU/g |
| E. coli O157:H7 (STEC) | Absent in 25 g |
| Salmonella | ≤ 1 positive/10 samples per lot |
| Staphylococcus aureus | ≤ 500 CFU/g |

|---|---|

## 8. Environmental Monitoring Programs

For all food manufacturing facilities, FSSAI requires an **Environmental Monitoring Program (EMP)**:

- **Listeria monocytogenes sites:** RTE facilities must test ≥ 5 environmental sites monthly; any positive triggers corrective action
- **Salmonella sites:** Ingredient handling areas; monthly testing minimum
- **Indicator organisms (Total Plate Count, Enterobacteriaceae):** Process hygiene verification; frequency based on risk assessment (minimum weekly for high-risk areas)

### Corrective Actions for Environmental Positives

| Organism Found | Immediate Action | Long-term Action |
|---|---|---|
| Listeria in Zone 1 (direct contact) | Hold all product; cease production; intensive sanitize; retest | Root cause analysis; SOP revision; re-training |
| Listeria in Zone 2 (adjacent to line) | Intensive sanitize affected area; 3 consecutive negatives before production | Enhanced monitoring; frequency doubled for 90 days |
| Salmonella any zone | Hold product; sanitize; retest | Product risk assessment; supplier audit |

## 9. Sampling Plans and Acceptance Criteria

FSSAI applies the **ICMSF (International Commission on Microbiological Specifications for Foods)** 2-class and 3-class sampling plans:

- **n** = number of sample units
- **c** = maximum number of defective units acceptable
- **m** = lower limit (acceptable); **M** = upper limit (rejection threshold)

For Listeria monocytogenes in RTE foods:
- n = 5, c = 0 (zero tolerance plan)
- Any positive = lot rejection

For TPC in pasteurized milk:
- n = 5, c = 2, m = 30,000 CFU/mL, M = 300,000 CFU/mL
